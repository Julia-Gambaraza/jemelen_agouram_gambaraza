Finding dead neurons in GMIC

A neuron is considered dead when it stops responding altogether: its output stays at zero for every input, regardless of what the network is asked to process. This is a common side effect of ReLU activations. Once a neuron's weights are pushed into a region where the gradient can no longer flow through it, the neuron stops learning and never recovers, quietly wasting a slice of the model's capacity.

In this piece, we open up GMIC, a two-scale model that reads mammograms by combining a global view of the breast with local, high-resolution glimpses of suspicious regions, and map out which of its neurons have gone dark. Where in the network does this happen, and what does it cost the model?
